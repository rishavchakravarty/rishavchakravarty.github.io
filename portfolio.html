Conceptualization and Initial Development

In the beginning phase of the Eventualist project, I endeavored to create an AI-driven calendar application specifically tailored for Computer Science students. My goal was to integrate psychological insights into technological solutions, thereby enhancing time management and learning efficiency. I was driven by the vision of an app that not only streamlined scheduling but also delved into the cognitive and behavioral patterns of its users, offering intelligent, personalized recommendations.

Research and Development

My journey into the development of Eventualist began with extensive user-centric research. I employed observational studies and interviews, focusing on understanding the cognitive and emotional challenges faced by Computer Science students. This approach was crucial in gaining insights into their daily struggles with scheduling and time management, influenced by their unique academic and personal responsibilities. My background in psychology played a key role in this phase, as I used principles of human behavior and cognitive processes to inform the app's design and features.

Design and Prototyping

The design phase of Eventualist was marked by the creation of detailed personas, such as Marka Carter, a vibrant CS junior, who represented the core challenges and needs of the target user group. These personas were instrumental in guiding the app's design, ensuring that it catered to a diverse range of psychological and emotional needs. I focused on creating storyboards and wireframes that emphasized not just the functional aspects of the app but also its emotional and ecological implications in the users' lives.

Prototype Development and User Feedback

In developing the prototype, I incorporated features like a real-time dashboard, personalized notifications, and multilingual support, considering the diverse cognitive and linguistic needs of the user base. The pilot tests were a critical component, where I gathered feedback to refine user interaction, adding features for enhanced personalization and inclusivity. This phase was particularly challenging and rewarding, as it involved balancing technical feasibility with user-centric design.

Analytical Evaluation and Refinement

The final phase involved a detailed analytical evaluation of the prototype. I conducted heuristic evaluations focusing on usability, task management, and user interface efficiency. This evaluation was crucial in understanding how the app's design impacted user experience, particularly in terms of cognitive load and emotional response. Based on this feedback, I made several refinements to the app, enhancing its intuitiveness and user-friendliness.

Project: Self-Driving Game Exploration

Role: Developer

Embarking on a cutting-edge journey in the realm of artificial intelligence and self-driving technology, I undertook the development of an innovative machine learning program. This program leveraged the power of PyAutoGUI, numpy, and PIL (Python Imaging Library) to delve into the exciting world of autonomous vehicles and game-based learning.

The primary objective of this project was to explore the capabilities of AI-driven self-driving models through the medium of video games. By creating an environment where virtual cars navigated game scenarios autonomously, I aimed to simulate real-world driving challenges and apply machine learning techniques to enhance their decision-making and control.

The project commenced with an in-depth study of PyAutoGUI, numpy, and PIL, which served as the core technologies for interfacing with game environments, handling data, and processing images. PyAutoGUI allowed for programmatic control of the game, while numpy provided essential numerical operations, and PIL facilitated image analysis and manipulation.

Machine Learning in Gaming

Exploring the intersection of machine learning and gaming presented a fascinating challenge. I ventured into the world of reinforcement learning, implementing algorithms that allowed virtual cars to learn through trial and error within the game environment. Reinforcement learning models were trained to maximize in-game rewards, leading to increasingly skillful and autonomous behavior.

Data Handling and Image Analysis

The project also involved extensive data handling and image analysis. Numpy played a pivotal role in processing game data and facilitating numerical operations, while PIL enabled the program to analyze in-game screenshots, recognize patterns, and make informed decisions based on visual cues.

Continuous Improvement

One of the most exciting aspects of this project was witnessing the continuous improvement of the virtual cars' driving skills. Through machine learning and adaptive algorithms, the AI-driven vehicles honed their abilities over time, mastering the art of self-driving and demonstrating the potential of AI in real-world applications.
